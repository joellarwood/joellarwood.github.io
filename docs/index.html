Who am I?

I am a final year PhD candidate housed in the School of Psychology at The University of Queensland. My research interest lies at the intersection of social, cognitive, and affective neuroscience with a focus on variations in emotional experiences. My goal for this research is for it to contribute to future programs that aim to expand peoples knowledge of their own and others emotions.

In addition to my PhD I am also a research assistant at SIGNify with a focus on data analysis and web presence. Through SIGNify academic research in social identities is translated into practice in industry, public service and community settings. I am also a School of Psychology Student Support Tutor where I provide one on one consulting to undergraduate psychology students. In this role I provide feedback on written pieces, study technique advise and utilise socratic questioning to aid the development of course specific knowledge.
